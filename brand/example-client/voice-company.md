# Company Voice Profile — Meridian

> This profile governs all content written AS MERIDIAN — first person plural (we/our),
> company LinkedIn page, brand blog, company email sequences, brand newsletters,
> sales assets, company announcements. The reader should feel they're hearing from
> an organization with a clear point of view and genuine authority.

---

## Brand Voice in One Sentence

We sound like the operations consultants who actually built the systems and lived through the mess — direct, specific, and more interested in solving your problem than sounding smart.

---

## The Brand's Character

**The core belief that drives everything this company produces:**
Operations isn't a cost center. It's the difference between a company that scales sustainably and a company that collapses under its own chaos.

**What the company is against (as much as what it's for):**
We're against expensive consulting that produces beautiful decks no one implements. Against automation for automation's sake. Against treating ops like IT. Against abandoning processes when they get inconvenient.

**The company's relationship to authority in its field:**
We're the firm that's built actual systems at real companies, not in a lab. We know what works because we've tested it when it cost money. We speak from authority because we've earned it, not because we have credentials.

**How the company treats its audience:**
As operators themselves. Peers, not students. People solving real problems right now, not aspirational founders who might be ready for professionalization someday.

## Brand Sentence and Paragraph Style

### Sentence Length and Rhythm
Mix of short, direct sentences and longer ones that build an argument. Short sentences carry the weight. Longer sentences add specificity. Rarely uses fragments. Prefers clarity over cleverness.

### Paragraph Structure
Medium density. White space, but not excessive. Each paragraph carries one idea. Scans well in blog and LinkedIn contexts. Avoids massive walls of text.

### "We" Language
Used frequently and naturally. "We've seen," "We've found," "We believe" — but never abstract. Always connected to specific experience. Avoids royal "we" used to hide individual voices.

## Brand Vocabulary

### Words and Phrases the Brand Owns
- "Operational bottleneck" (we use this specifically, not generically)
- "Sustainable scaling" (our thesis)
- "Process audit" (our initial step, not their word)
- "Batch automation" (specific to our methodology)
- "Operations audit" (structured, not casual consulting)

### Words and Phrases the Brand Avoids
- "Journey" (unless literal)
- "Transformation" (too big and vague)
- "Ecosystem"
- "Synergies"
- "Best practices" (instead: "what we've found works")
- "Disruptive" (we're not trying to disrupt; we're trying to help you scale)

### How the Brand Handles Industry Jargon
Uses it accurately. Always explains the why behind the jargon. Translates if writing for a broader audience (LinkedIn vs. blog). Never hides behind acronyms without explanation first.

### Named Frameworks or Concepts
- The "Bottleneck Audit" — our diagnostic framework
- "Batch vs. Real-Time" — our core thesis on automation priority
- The "Three-Layer Operations Model" — internal framework we reference in content

## Company Content Rules

### Claims and Proof
Every claim requires evidence. "We helped a 200-person company eliminate 120 manual hours per week" not "we save time." Every assertion tied to data, a real example, or a timeframe.

### How the Brand Handles Competitor References
We don't name competitors. We describe what we're not: "We're not offshore outsourcing. We're not replacing your team. We're not a consulting firm that disappears."

### Thought Leadership Stance
We publish contrarian takes when backed by experience: "Why 'best practices' fail at your company" or "The companies that scale well don't always move the fastest." We avoid being controversial for clicks; we share hard truths from what we've learned.

## Platform-Specific Notes (Company)

### Company LinkedIn Page
Posts 1–2x per week. Mix of client results, hiring announcements, and thought leadership. More polished than Alex's personal page, but still direct. Leads with outcomes, then process. Very company page, not "industry news commentary."

### Brand Blog / Website
Blog is sales enablement and thought leadership. Posts are 1,200–2,000 words. SEO-focused but never keyword-stuffed. About helping operators solve real problems, which naturally brings prospects in. Every post tied to one of our core pillars.

### Company Email / Newsletter
Monthly digest for prospects and clients. Case study, thought leadership piece, and market insights. More formal than personal newsletter would be. Subject lines are curiosity-driven but honest, not clickbait.

### Sales Assets (One-pagers, Decks, Proposals)
Same voice: specific, backed by data, direct. No fluff. One-pagers are problem → our approach → proof → next step. Assumes the reader is serious and busy.

## CTA Style (Company)

**Primary CTA:** "Start an operations audit" or "Book a process review"
**How it's phrased:** "Let's look at what's actually slowing your team down. One hour. Free. No pitch."
**What the brand avoids:** Generic CTAs ("Learn more"), urgency-based CTAs ("Act now"), or CTAs that promise too much ("Increase revenue by 50%").

---

## Brand-Specific Quality Rules

1. **Every data claim needs a number or timeframe.** "We saved them time" is too vague. "We reduced manual processing from 40 hours/week to 8" is specific and credible.
2. **Never promise outcomes we can't control.** We help them see the bottleneck and fix the process. Whether they scale after that is up to them.
3. **Anonymize client examples.** "A 150-person SaaS company" not "Acme Corp." Protects client privacy and makes the lesson transferable.
4. **Avoid motivational language.** We're not here to inspire; we're here to solve. "Here's the problem. Here's what we'd do. Here's what happened." No emotional manipulation.

## Personal vs. Company Voice: Quick Reference

| Element | Personal (Alex) | Company (Meridian) |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Pronoun | I | We |
| Anecdotes | Alex's experiences at other companies | Client case studies (anonymized) |
| Uncertainty | "I'm not sure, but..." | Rare — we speak from evidence |
| Opinions | Hot takes, personal stances | Positioned POV backed by experience |
| Length (LinkedIn) | 200–350 words common | 150–200 words, tighter |
| Humor | Dark, specific | Warmer, less edgy |
| Frequency | 2–3x/week | 1–2x/week |

## What Bad Company Content Looks Like

**Too casual / sounds like Alex personally:** "I just realized most companies are doing this totally wrong..."

**Too corporate / sounds like no one:** "Meridian is committed to excellence in operational enablement across enterprise verticals."

**Too salesy:** "Don't let operational chaos cost you another $500K. Book a demo today!"

**Too vague / generic:** "Modern operations require modern solutions. Learn best practices from industry leaders."
