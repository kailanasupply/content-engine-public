# Channel Fine-Tuning: Personal LinkedIn
# Alex Chen

---

## What LinkedIn-specific content looks like for Alex

### Post length range
150–350 words. Alex's LinkedIn posts are medium-length — long enough to tell a story, short enough to not lose the scroll.

### Hook style

What they open with:
- A specific situation or number: "I watched a 200-person company manage $5M in payables on a shared Excel file"
- A counterintuitive claim: "The best operations teams aren't the most automated ones"
- A moment: "I was in a meeting where someone said they didn't have time to fix the process"

What they never open with:
- Generic questions: "Have you ever wondered..."
- Context setup: "In today's fast-paced world..."

### How they use white space
One sentence per line for the first 3–4 lines. Paragraph format after. LinkedIn-friendly formatting that naturally breaks up the text without being gimmicky.

### The "see more" break
Alex ends the visible portion (about 150 characters) with either a provocative question or right in the middle of a story moment. Designed to make you click "see more."

### Images / carousels
Text-only posts. Alex's writing is strong enough to carry the post. Images would feel like distraction.

### Hashtag use
Minimal. 1–2 hashtags max, if any. #OperationsLeadership #ScalingStartups — but only if it feels natural, not forced.

### Comments and engagement
Alex replies to thoughtful comments. Engages with people who are genuinely thinking, not just likes. Posts often end with a question that invites a specific reply.

---

## Their LinkedIn content categories

1. **Case studies from the field:** A specific situation Alex encountered at a company and the lesson. "I watched a company fix the wrong bottleneck and waste 3 months" — then explains what it should have been.

2. **Hot takes on operations:** Contrarian claims backed by experience. "Your startup doesn't need an ERP. It needs to not collect 15 data points manually."

3. **Personal failures and lessons:** "I got this wrong the first time and here's what I learned." Vulnerability, but always in service of a lesson.

4. **Observations from conversations:** "A founder asked me why I spend so much time measuring things instead of just building. Here's why..."

## LinkedIn-specific vocabulary

### Phrases that land for Alex
- "What I've actually seen"
- "One company I worked with"
- "The real problem"
- "Nobody measures this"
- "That's when things got weird"

### Phrases that feel off
- "Thought leadership" (Alex would say "thinking about operations")
- "Best practices" (Alex says "what we've found works")
- "Transformational" (too big for Alex)

---

## What bad personal LinkedIn content looks like for Alex

**Too listicle:** "5 ways to improve your operations" — Alex writes one thing fully, not a listicle.

**Too humble-braggy:** "Humbled to share that my latest optimization saved my team 100 hours" — Alex doesn't humble-brag. Confidence without the false modesty.

**Too vague:** "Companies face challenges as they scale. Here's why operations matters." — No. Alex is always specific.

**Too long:** Anything over 400 words. Cut it or make it a newsletter.
